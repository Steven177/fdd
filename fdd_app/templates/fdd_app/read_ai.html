The Task of Object Detection

With object detection machines can create visual understanding.

Input: Image

Output: The model can detect ~100 different object classes. For each object detected in an image, the model outputs:

  • Bounding box
  • Class label
  • Confidence score

Limitations

Blur

Blurry images or rapid movement between frames due to motion can lead to errors.

Clutter

Computer vision systems tend to struggle with cluttered images.

Lighting

Poor lighting or high contrast illumination (e.g. nighttime) may lead to wrong predictions.

Object size

If objects become very small, the vision system may struggle to detect it.

Occlusions

Partially occluded objects may not be detected.

Resolution

Images of low resolution could lead to errors.

Similar objects

Objects that are similar in shape can be difficult to distinguish for the computer vision system.
